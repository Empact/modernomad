Short-Term Accommodations

Guests are a cornerstone of our house and we love having new (and familiar) people stay with us. This isn’t your typical B&B – it’s more of an adopted family. We’re working on the hypothesis that density breeds ideas and collaborations, and that’s a big part of what we hope to foster here.

We know and expect that guests are usually traveling for specific projects and collaborations. Similarly, residents are working on their own projects most days. But, just like staying with a friend or family mbber, it’s nice to hang out a bit, too! So, we request that you do make a little bit of time to interact with people in the house, and for serendipity to occur.

Events

To aid in your enthusiasm, we try to keep the house lively, fun and interesting, with anything from casual chats in the evenings to movie nights, dinners, performances and salons.

Projects

As a community house there are also many ways you can contribute! Everything that happens here is a result of the people, and that includes you. Instigate or help out at a big meal; bring a treat from your hometown; play music; sing us a song; host a discussion about a topic you are experienced in, passionate about, or perplexed by; bring wine or spirits (bonus points for homebrew); unload the dishwasher (we’ll forgive you for putting things back in weird places); arrange chairs or organize a surface – take pride in the house; leave a copy of a book you enjoyed; fix, build, or document something; teach a short class related to your craft, ask someone how their day was...

Home

Finally, one of our most important traditions is Sunday night dinner: our weekly community meal with new and familiar faces, boisterous conversation, many helping hands, and most importantly, our way to say Thanks to all the amazing people we know. It’s a time for the “in-between” time, when there’s no set topic or purpose except sharing space with other interesting folks. If it’s at all possible for you to attend, we strongly encourage you to do so (and will make sad faces if you don’t).
